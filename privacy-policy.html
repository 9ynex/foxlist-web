🦊 Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026

FoxList is a list-sharing app built around one idea: no accounts. You create a list, share its link, and anyone with that link can see and edit it. This page explains what that means for your data.

Information we collect

  • List content. Items, categories, notes, prices, and due dates you add to a list are stored in our database so the list can sync in real time across everyone viewing it.
  • Display name. The name you enter is stored only on your device and sent along with items you add, so collaborators know who added what.
  • An anonymous device identifier. The app signs you in anonymously behind the scenes — no email, no password — purely so our database can tell requests apart. It isn't linked to your name or any personal information.
  • Presence. While you have a list open, a temporary marker lets other viewers see that someone is currently looking at it. It's cleared automatically when you close the list or disconnect.

What stays on your device

Saved templates, your recently-opened lists, and item suggestions (built from things you've typed before) are stored locally on your device only. We never see them, and they aren't synced anywhere.

What we don't do

  • No account creation, email, or password required or collected.
  • No advertising, analytics, or tracking of your activity.
  • No selling, renting, or sharing your data with third parties.

How sharing works

A list's link is its access key — anyone who has it can view and edit that list. There's no login to restrict access by identity, so treat a list's link the way you'd treat a shared password: only send it to people you want collaborating on that list.

Data retention and deletion

A list and everything in it is kept until its creator deletes it (the "Delete List" option removes the list, its items, and its categories from our database). Data stored only on your device — templates, history, your display name — can be cleared at any time by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling it.

Children's privacy

FoxList is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page.
